Gurramkonda fort

Overview of gurramkonda fort

    • Gurramkonda Fort is a historic hill fort located in Gurramkonda village, Annamayya District, Andhra Pradesh (previously part of Chittoor District). Built atop a steep hill about 500 feet high, the fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding plains. The name "Gurramkonda" means "Horse Hill" — “Gurram” = horse, “Konda” = hill — believed to reference the use of horses to climb or guard the steep terrain.
  • 🔍 Historical Importance
  • The fort dates back to the 14th century, built during the rule of the Vijayanagara Empire. It was later occupied by various rulers, including: Nawabs of Kadapa Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan Eventually came under British control. The fort played a strategic military role due to its naturally defensible position
  • 🏛️ Architecture & Layout
  • Built using stone, lime, and mud, the fort blends Indo-Islamic and South Indian styles. Key structures within or near the fort: Rangini Mahal – an old palace structure, once used by royalty. Tomb of Mir Raza Ali Khan – maternal uncle of Tipu Sultan. Temples – small, centuries-old Hindu temples still present in and around the hill. Mosques and Islamic structures – added during Muslim rule. Fort walls, bastions, and remnants of old gateways are still visible. Steep natural terrain forms part of its defense; three sides are surrounded by deep slopes.
