history

Sri Veerabhadra Swamy Temple is located on Devakuta Parvatham, a hillock on an islet in the middle of the Godavari River near Pattiseema in West Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh. The temple is dedicated to Lord Veerabhadra Swamy, an incarnation of Lord Shiva, who is said to have been born from Shiva's hair to avenge the insult to Goddess Sati by Daksha Prajapati. According to legend, after killing Daksha, Veerabhadra performed the fierce Rudra Tandava dance on this hill, which was later calmed by Sage Agastya. The temple also features a massive monolithic Nandi statue and shrines for Goddess Bhadrakali and other deities. It is part of the Pancha Kasi Kshetras mentioned in the Skanda Purana and is a significant pilgrimage site, celebrating festivals like Maha Shivaratri with great devotion.
