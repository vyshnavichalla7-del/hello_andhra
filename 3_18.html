sri palikondeswara swamy temple

history

  • Location:
    • The Sri Pallikondeswara Swamy Temple is a unique and revered Hindu temple located in Surutapalli, approximately 2 km from Uthukottai on the Chennai–Periyapalayam–Tirupati road. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva in his reclining posture, symbolizing the Bhoga Sayana form, where Shiva lies on the lap of Goddess Parvati. This depiction is rare among Shiva temples and is akin to Lord Vishnu's Anantasayana form.
  • Legends:
    • The main deity, Pallikondeswara, is shown resting peacefully, symbolizing divine rest and cosmic balance. Surrounding the main deity are sculptures and images of various gods and sages like Ganesha, Kartikeya, Surya (Sun God), Chandra (Moon God), Indra, and sages like Narada and Agastya. The temple is also believed to have stones that bear the footprints of Lava and Kusha, the sons of Lord Rama, connecting it to the epic Ramayana.

    architecture

    The temple dates back to the Vijayanagara period (14th century) and was later renovated in the 19th century.
