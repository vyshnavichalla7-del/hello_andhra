Muradi Anjaneya Swami Temple

History

The temple dates back to the 16th century and was consecrated by the famous saint and spiritual leader Sri Vyasaraya, who was the Rajaguru of Vijayanagara Empire King Krishnadevaraya (1509-1530). Sri Vyasaraya is known to have installed 732 Hanuman idols across South India, with three major temples in Anantapur district, including Muradi Anjaneya, Nettikanti Anjaneya (Kasapuram), and Nemakallu Anjaneya temples. The name "Muradi" originates from the Kannada word meaning "three feet," referencing the size of the Hanuman statue. Historical belief holds that a precious stone is embedded in one of the deity's eyes, increasing its sanctity.

Visiting Timings

  • The temple is generally open from 5:00 AM to 8:00 PM to accommodate daily worship and special rituals.
  • Festive days, especially those in the month of Shravanamasam, see large crowds with special poojas and offerings.
  • Daily rituals include Nitya Pooja, Abhishekam, Archana, and specific observances on Tuesdays, Sundays, and during Shashti.
