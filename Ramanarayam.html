Ramanarayanam

History

Inaugurated in 2014, the temple was envisioned by the late Sri Narayanam Narasimha Murthy, founder of the NCS Charitable Trust. Spanning over 15 acres on Korukonda Road, it is designed in the shape of a bow and arrow, symbolizing the epic tale of the Ramayana. The temple's architecture is a blend of traditional Hindu temple design and modern elements, making it a unique structure in the region.

Ramanarayanam serves as a center for spiritual learning and cultural enrichment. It aims to propagate the values of truth and righteousness as depicted in the Ramayana. The temple has become a major tourist attraction in the Uttarandhra region, drawing over 2.4 million visitors within 18 months of its establishment.
