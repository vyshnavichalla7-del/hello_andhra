Borra caves

Overview

Borra Caves are spectacular limestone caves located in the Ananthagiri Hills of the Araku Valley, Visakhapatnam district, Andhra Pradesh. Situated at an elevation of about 705 meters (2,313 feet) above sea level, these caves are among the largest and deepest in India, extending to a depth of around 80 meters (260 feet). Key Details: The Borra Caves were discovered in 1807 by British geologist William King. They are estimated to be over 1-2 million years old and formed by erosion of limestone deposits by the Gosthani River. The caves are famous for stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations resembling shapes like Lord Shiva-Parvati, Rishi’s beard, mother-child, crocodile, human brains, tiger, and cow’s udder. Inside the caves, there is a natural Shiva Lingam revered by locals, with a small temple built near the entrance for worship.
