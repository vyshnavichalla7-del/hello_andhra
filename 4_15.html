sri kodandarama temple

History

  • location

    • Town: Buchireddypalem District: Nellore, Andhra Pradesh Nearby major city: Nellore city is about 25 km away

  • legend

    • The main deities are Sri Kodandarama Swamy (Lord Rama) along with Sita Devi and Lakshmana Swamy. Additional shrines inside include Chaturbhuja Lakshmi, Lakshmi Narasimha, Andal, and Hanuman (Anjaneya).

    .

  • architecture

    • Built around 1784 AD by Sri Dodla Rami Reddy, who is said to have had a divine vision of Lord Rama requesting a temple in Buchireddypalem. The temple has been maintained by the Dodla family ever since, who also supported its upkeep and rituals.

    The temple complex spreads over about 2 acres. It features a large, beautifully decorated rajagopuram (main tower) with sculptures of various gods and goddesses. There is a temple pond (koneru) inside the premises used by devotees for holy dips. Spacious mantapams (pillared halls) and courtyards allow devotees to gather comfortably.

Visiting Timings

  • Opens early in the morning, around 5:00 AM.
  • Closes at night, around 9:00 PM.
  • Daily pujas, abhishekams, and archana are performed regularly.
