Victory at sea memorial

Overview

The Victory at Sea Memorial in Visakhapatnam is a war memorial dedicated to the Indian Navy and the sailors of the Eastern Naval Command who participated in the 1971 Indo-Pakistani War. It was constructed in 1996 and is located on Beach Road near Ramakrishna Beach and the INS Kurusura Submarine Museum. Key Highlights: The memorial commemorates Indiaâ€™s naval victory over Pakistan in the 1971 war, particularly the sinking of the Pakistani submarine PNS Ghazi off the Visakhapatnam coast. It honors the bravery and sacrifices of the Indian Navy personnel. The monument features a permanently burning pyre in tribute to martyrs, accompanied by displays of missiles, fighter planes, torpedoes, and tankers. The towering obelisk has the inscription: "The nation that forgets her defenders needs no ancestors."
