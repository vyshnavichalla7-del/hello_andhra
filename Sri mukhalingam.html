Sri Mukhalingam

History

Sri Mukhalingam Temple, located in Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh, is an ancient and sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is believed to have been built between the 7th and 8th centuries during the reign of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, making it one of the oldest Shiva temples in the region. The temple is famous for its unique architecture, featuring beautifully carved stone structures in the traditional Dravidian style, showcasing the artistic skills of ancient artisans. The name “Mukhalingam” refers to the Shiva Linga with four faces (Mukhams), representing the four directions, symbolizing Lord Shiva’s omnipresence. Historically, Sri Mukhalingam was a significant center for Shaivism and pilgrimage, attracting devotees from various regions. The temple has been an important cultural and religious landmark, representing centuries of devotion, architecture, and spiritual traditions in Andhra Pradesh. Sri Mukhalingam is home to one of the ancient Shiva temples in Andhra Pradesh. It is renowned for its grand temple complex and historical importance, dating back to the 8th century. The temple architecture features intricate carvings and a large temple tank. Located about 45 km from Srikakulam town.
