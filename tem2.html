Chenna Kesava Swamy Temple

History

The Sri Prasanna Chennakesava Swamy Temple in Ongole was built in the 16th century by the feudatory kings Rama Bhadhra Raju and Raghupathy Raju, under the Vijayanagara Kingdom, on a hill in Ongole. The temple is dedicated to Chenna Kesava Perumal, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. While the current temple dates to the 16th century, its origins are traced further back to 1113 AD during the Haihaya kings' period, and its development was influenced by the Cholas and Hoysalas.
