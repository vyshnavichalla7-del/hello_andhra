sri prasanna venkateswara swamy temple

History

  • location

    • Kondabitragunta village, near Bitragunta and Kavali, in Nellore district.

  • legend

    • Lord Venkateswara (here called Prasanna Venkateswara Swamy) appears in a unique posture. The conch (Shankha) and discus (Chakra) in His hands are in reversed positions compared to the regular Tirumala idol. Nickname: Often referred to as "Kaliyuga Vaikuntham" (the Vaikuntha of this age)

  • architecture

    • Believed to have been built in 1356 AD during the Chola dynasty rule. Spiritual Significance: Known as Bilakoota Kshetram, this is a powerful place where Sage Narada is said to have done penance after being cursed.

Visiting Timings

  • 5:45 AM to 8:00 PM
