Ksheera ramalingeswara swamy temple

history

Ksheera Ramalingeswara Swamy Temple, located in Palakollu town of West Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh, is one of the five Pancharama Kshetras sacred to Lord Shiva. The main deity, Shiva, is worshiped as Ksheera Ramalingeswara Swamy, with the unique milky white Shiva Lingam, believed to have been installed by Lord Vishnu. The temple's tower is one of the tallest in Andhra Pradesh, standing 120 feet high with nine floors, constructed during the 9th century under the reign of Chalukya Bheema. The temple complex houses shrines for various deities and features exquisite Dravidian architecture including 72 granite pillars and beautiful fresco paintings. It holds great religious significance, with a belief that staying one day here is equivalent to spending a year in Varanasi.
