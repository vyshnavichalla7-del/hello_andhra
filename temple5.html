Sri Yogananda Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Devasthanam

History

The temple is one of the Pancha Narasimha Kshetras located on the holy banks of the Krishna River in Vedadri village, Krishna district, Andhra Pradesh. According to legend, Lord Vishnu took the Matsya Avatar to retrieve the stolen Vedas from the demon Somakasura. The Vedas personified requested the Lord to reside on their heads after he killed the demon Hiranyakashipu. Lord Vishnu appeared as Narasimha, killing Hiranyakashipu and residing as five different forms collectively called Pancha Narasimha Murthis: Jwala Narasimha, Salagrama Narasimha, Yogananda Narasimha (the main form at Vedadri), Lakshmi Narasimha, and Veera Narasimha. Sage Rishya Shringi consecrated the swayambhu (self-manifested) Deity of Sri Yogananda Narasimha Swamy found in a cave on Vedadri hill. The temple flourished during the Vijayanagara Empire and is famous for its mythological ambiance along the Krishna River, offering relief to devotees from ailments and other afflictions.
