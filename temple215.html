Nemakallu anjaneya temple

History

The temple, along with the related Nettikanti and Muradi Anjaneya temples, was consecrated by the saint Vyasaraya during the Vijayanagara Empire under King Krishnadevaraya (around 1509–1530 AD). Vyasaraya installed 732 Hanuman idols across South India, with many of them in Anantapur district, signaling this temple’s distinctive historic and devotional significance. It is believed that a precious stone is embedded in one of the deity's eyes, adding to the temple's sanctity and spiritual aura. The temple has continuity in traditional worship by local families and temple authorities, and it is a part of the triad of famous Hanuman temples in the district that devotees visit, especially during the Shravanamasam month

Visiting Timings

  • The temple is open from 5:00 AM to 8:00 PM, accommodating daily rituals and special occasions.
  • Significant worship occurs on Tuesdays, Sundays, and during special festivals like Hanuman Jayanti and Shashti.
