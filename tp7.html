Overview

Dolphin’s Nose is a prominent hill in Visakhapatnam, situated between Yarada Beach and Gangavaram Port, named for its natural shape resembling a dolphin’s nose. It stands about 358 meters (1,175 feet) above sea level, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city, harbor, and the Bay of Bengal. Key Details: The hill is a major landmark and popular viewpoint for tourists and locals. It houses the Dolphin’s Nose Lighthouse, a 19-meter tall masonry tower with black and white bands, operational since June 15, 1957. The lighthouse emits a powerful light visible up to around 65 kilometers at sea, helping guide ships entering Visakhapatnam Port. The site includes a park with diverse flora, ideal for picnics and relaxation. The location has historical significance, used by British forces as a military camp. Nearby, there is a Hindu temple, a church, and a mosque.
