kondakarla ava lake and bird sanctuary

Overview

Kondakarla Ava Lake and Bird Sanctuary is a notable eco-tourism destination located about 49 km from Visakhapatnam in Kondakarla village, Atchutapuram Mandal, Andhra Pradesh. It is recognized for housing one of the largest freshwater lakes in the country, spread across approximately 1,832 acres, nestled along the foothills of the Eastern Ghats and surrounded by magnanimous hills. Key Features: The lake is shallow (about 6 to 7 feet deep) and supports a diverse aquatic ecosystem, including around twenty-five fish species. The bird sanctuary is home to several resident and migratory bird species such as Grey Herons, Red Whiskered Bulbuls, Black Drongos, and Pheasant Tailed Jacanas. The sanctuary features a unique and endangered type of wet evergreen forest. Visitors can enjoy boating in traditional bamboo boats made by local villagers, providing an authentic and memorable experience.
