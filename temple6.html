Sri achanta rameswara swamy temple

history

sri Achanta Rameswaram Swamy Temple, also known as Achanteswara Swamy Temple, is located in Achanta town in West Godavari district, Andhra Pradesh. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped as Rameswara Swamy or Achanteswara, with a self-manifested (Swayambhu) Shiva Linga. According to legend, Lord Rama, on his return to Ayodhya after killing the demon Ravana, worshipped Shiva here to absolve the sin of killing a Brahmin, hence the name Ramalingeswara. The temple architecture owes much to the Eastern Chalukya period (700-1130 AD), featuring a towering five-storied gopuram with intricate carvings. The temple complex also hosts shrines for other deities, and festivals like Maha Shivaratri are celebrated with grandeur, attracting devotees from far and wide.
