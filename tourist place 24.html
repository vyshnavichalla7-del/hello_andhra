Nimmalakunta ananthapur

History

Nimmalakunta is a small village in the Anantapur district of Andhra Pradesh, famous for its centuries-old traditional art form called Tholu Bommalata or leather shadow puppetry. This ancient folk art uses intricately crafted and vividly painted leather puppets to narrate stories from Indian epics like the Ramayana and Mahabharata. Tholu Bommalata means “dance of leather puppets.” Puppets are made from specially treated goat hide. The art was originally practiced by migrating communities from Maharashtra about 700 years ago and took root in Nimmalakunta around 90 years ago. Puppeteers from this village are nationally and internationally recognized for their skill. The tradition is slowly evolving into making decorative leather craft items like lampshades, wall hangings, and miniature puppets due to changes in entertainment preferences.
