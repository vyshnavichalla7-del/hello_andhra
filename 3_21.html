sri kundaleswara swamy temple

history

  • Location:
    • The Sri Kulandeshwara Swamy Temple is a revered Hindu temple located in Kattamanchi, a village in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, known here as Kulandeshwara Swamy.
  • Legends:
    • The temple is not only a place of worship but also a symbol of faith and resilience. The story of the Sthala Vriksha's rebirth and the installation of Vriksha Ganapati beneath it reflects the belief that divine blessings can restore and rejuvenate

    Sthala Vriksha: The temple is associated with a sacred tree, known as the Sthala Vriksha. According to local legends, this tree was destroyed due to natural calamities. However, a sapling of the same tree began to grow beside the original, symbolizing resilience and divine grace. A Vinayaka idol was installed beneath this sapling, and the deity came to be known as Vriksha Ganapati.

    architecture

    Temple Structure: The temple features traditional Dravidian architecture, with intricately carved pillars and a towering Shikhara (spire) above the sanctum sanctorum.
