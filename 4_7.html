History

  • location

    • The Sri Mallikarjuna Swamy Kamakshi Tayee Temple in Jonnawada, located approximately 10 km west of Nellore, Andhra Pradesh

  • legend

    • Lord Mallikarjuna Swamy (a form of Lord Shiva) Goddess Kamakshi Tayee (manifestation of Goddess Parvati) Other deities include Sri Lakshmi Ganapathi Swamy, Sri Subrahmanyeswara Swamy, Sri Tarakeswara Swamy, and Sri Kasi Annapurneswari Ammavaru. History: The temple is believed to have been established around 1150 A.D. It is associated with Sage Kashyapa, who performed a significant yagna (sacrificial ritual) at this site.

    .

  • architecture

    • . The temple features a grand Raja Gopuram (main tower) adorned with intricate carvings and topped with five golden kalasams (decorative finials). The sanctum sanctorum houses the self-manifested idols of Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Kamakshi Tayee. The temple complex contains separate shrines for other deities, all built in traditional South Indian temple architectural style. Adjacent to the temple is the sacred Kashyapa Theertham pond, believed to cleanse devotees of their sins.
