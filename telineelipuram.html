Telineelipuram Bird Sanctuary

Overview

Established in 1986, the sanctuary spans approximately 3 acres and serves as a vital habitat for over 100 bird species, including the Painted Stork and Spot-billed Pelican. These migratory birds travel thousands of kilometers from regions like Siberia, Germany, Australia, and Malaysia to breed and nest here during the cooler months from September to March. The sanctuary provides a safe haven for these species, offering abundant food and suitable nesting sites. Migratory Bird Watching: Witnessing the arrival of thousands of migratory birds, including Painted Storks and Spot-billed Pelicans, is a highlight for visitors. Nesting Behavior: Observing the unique nesting habits of these birds, such as their preference for tamarind and mesquite trees, offers insight into their reproductive behaviors. Ecological Diversity: The sanctuary is home to a variety of resident species, including Grey Herons, Whimbrels, and Bar-headed Geese, making it a paradise for bird watchers.
