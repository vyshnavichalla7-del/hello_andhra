Sri Pydithalli Ammavari temple

History

Sri Pydithalli Ammavari Temple is dedicated to Goddess Pydithalli Ammavaru, believed to be the sister of King Vijaya Rama Raju of the Gajapati dynasty. In 1757, during the Battle of Bobbili, King Vijaya Rama Raju was killed. Upon hearing the tragic news, Pydithalli Ammavaru reportedly passed away and instructed that her idol be found near Pedda Cheruvu (Big Lake) in Vizianagaram. A temple was later constructed at that location, becoming a center of worship and cultural importance. Pydithalli Ammavaru is worshipped as the Grama Devata (village deity) of Vizianagaram. Devotees believe she protects them from evil, brings prosperity, and ensures peace in the region. The temple is especially famous for the grand Sirimanotsavam festival, celebrated annually after Dussehra, which attracts thousands of devotees.
