Bobbili Fort

History

Bobbili Fort was originally built in 1652 by Pedda Rayudu, a descendant of the Venkatagiri royal family, who established his rule in Bobbili. The fort became famous during the Battle of Bobbili in 1757, when Raja Gopalakrishna Rayudu and his small army bravely fought against the combined forces of the Vizianagaram rulers and French General Bussy. Though the fort fell, the battle is remembered for the unmatched courage and sacrifice of the Bobbili soldiers. Later, in the 19th century, Chinna Ranga Rao rebuilt the fort in Indo-Saracenic architectural style, giving it its present form.
