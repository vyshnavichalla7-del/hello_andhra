Umagiri Temple

History

The Umagiri Temple, also known as Uma Maheswara Temple, is an ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, located in Sarangapuram, Srikakulam, Andhra Pradesh. It is believed to have been constructed centuries ago and reflects the traditional Kalinga style of architecture with intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple holds a special place in local legends, as it is situated near Mahendragiri Peak in the Eastern Ghats, adding spiritual significance to its location. Over time, the temple became an important pilgrimage site, attracting devotees seeking blessings and tranquility in the serene surroundings. The temple is a prominent spiritual center for worship of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, symbolizing divine union and cosmic balance. It is part of the sacred landscape of Srikakulam, known for its connection to ancient Shaivite traditions. Devotees visit to perform special rituals like Abhishekam and Rudra Pooja, especially during festivals like Maha Shivaratri. Its location near Mahendragiri Peak is considered sacred, believed to be a place of divine energy and spiritual upliftment. The temple serves as a peaceful retreat for pilgrims seeking solace, meditation, and blessings from the divine couple.
