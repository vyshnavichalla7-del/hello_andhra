nagarjuna sagar dam

history

The Nagarjuna Sagar Dam is the world's largest masonry dam on the Krishna River between Telangana and Andhra Pradesh, built between 1955 and 1967 and officially inaugurated in 1967 by the then Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru, creating one of India's largest reservoirs for irrigation and hydropower. It serves as an early example of a multi-purpose project for the Green Revolution and is known for its high storage capacity and large number of gates
