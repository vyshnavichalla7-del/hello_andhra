Sri Jaladheeswara Aalayam

History

Sri Jaladheeswara Swamy Temple, also known as Sree Balaparvati Sametha Jaladheeswara Aalayam, is located in Ghantasala village, Krishna District, Andhra Pradesh. It is among the oldest temples in Andhra Pradesh, believed to have existed before the 2nd century A.D. Unique for having Lords Shiva and Parvati placed together on a single pedestal (peetam), a feature uncommon even among ancient temples like Srisailam and Srikalahasti. The peetam is believed to have been placed by Sage Agastya Maharshi. The temple's Shiva Lingam shares characteristics with temples such as Gudimallem, Amaravati, and Draksharamam. The temple also has shrines for Lord Nandeeshwara, Lord Ugra Narasimha, Lord Kalabhairava, Dwarapalakas, and Goddess Saraswati. According to Skanda Purana, visiting this temple grants the religious merit equivalent to seeing the twelve Jyotirlinga temples and eighteen Shakti Peethams. Devotees believe that the Jaladheeswara abhisheka theertham (holy water) has healing properties.
