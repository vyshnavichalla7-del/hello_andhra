sri vasavi kanyaka parameswari temple

History

  • location

    • Sri Vasavi Devi is worshipped primarily by the Arya Vysya (Komati) community across Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, and parts of South India. She is considered a form of Goddess Parvati and is revered as a symbol of peace, sacrifice, chastity, and dharma. Known as Kanyaka Parameswari — "Kanyaka" meaning virgin and "Parameswari" meaning Supreme Goddess.

  • legend

    • .Vasavi Devi was born to King Kusuma Sreshti and Queen Kusumamba, rulers of a prosperous Vysya kingdom in Penugonda. She was known for her spiritual wisdom, compassion, and beauty. When a powerful king demanded to marry her against her will, she chose self-sacrifice (Agni Pravesham — entering the sacred fire) rather than compromise her dharma and chastity. Along with her, 102 Gotra leaders also chose self-sacrifice, representing unity and sacrifice for virtue. After this divine act, she was venerated as Kanyaka Parameswari, an embodiment of Shakti.

    .

  • architecture

    • East-facing Garbhagriha (sanctum sanctorum) housing the idol of Vasavi Devi, often made of black granite or adorned in colorful attire and jewelry. Raja Gopuram (main tower) richly decorated with carvings of divine figures. Prakarams (corridors) for circumambulation. Mandapams (pillared halls) used for religious gatherings and festivals. Separate shrines for other deities like Sri Nageswara Swamy (Shiva), Ganesha, Navagrahas, and sometimes Astalakshmi.
