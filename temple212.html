History

Penukonda served as the second capital of the Vijayanagara Empire after the fall of Hampi in 1565 AD. The fort was originally established during the reign of King Virupanna and later gifted by King Bukka-I to his son Virupanna. It was a vital military and administrative hub under the Vijayanagara rulers including Krishnadevaraya. Over centuries, it witnessed control transition from Vijayanagara Empire to Golconda Sultans, Mysore Kingdom, and British colonial rule. The fort complex includes strategically designed walls, bastions, gateways, seven citadels, and structures blending Hindu and Islamic architectural styles. Temples in Penukonda Fort

Notable temples of Penukonda Fort are Hanuman Temple with an 11 feet tall idol at the entrance, Yoga Narasimhaswamy Temple, Kashi Vishwanatha Temple, and others which symbolize rich religious culture. The fort also has a significant mosque known as Babayya Darga and a Jain temple indicating a confluence of multiple religious traditions.
