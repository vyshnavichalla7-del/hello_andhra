History

  • location

    • The Bhagavan Sri Venkaiah Swamy Temple in Golagamudi, located approximately 15 km from Nellore city in Andhra Pradesh, is a revered spiritual center dedicated to Bhagavan Sri Venkaiah Swamy, a sage believed to be an avatar of Lord Dattatreya.

  • legend

    • Bhagavan Sri Venkaiah Swamy was a spiritual luminary who lived in Golagamudi during the mid-20th century. He attained Mahasamadhi (a state of conscious exit from the physical body) at this location, and his teachings continue to inspire devotees. He emphasized compassion, simplicity, and service to humanity, with the guiding principle: “Feed the hungry, shelter the distressed.”

    .

  • architecture

    • . The temple complex is designed to facilitate spiritual practices and community service. Key features include: Main Shrine (Samadhi Mandir): Houses the Samadhi (final resting place) of Bhagavan Sri Venkaiah Swamy. Prayer Hall: Spacious area for devotees to gather, meditate, and participate in rituals. Community Kitchen (Annadanam): Serves free meals to all visitors, embodying the Swamy's teachings of selfless service. Educational Facilities: Provides residential education for underprivileged children, ensuring that no child is denied a future due to poverty.
