Twlukonchi Bird Sanctuary

Overview

Migratory Bird Haven: Telukunchi acts as a crucial stopover for migratory birds, especially the Open-billed Storks, which arrive from regions such as Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bangladesh, Thailand, Myanmar, and Cambodia. These birds utilize the sanctuary's wetlands for breeding and feeding during the rainy season. Community Conservation Efforts: The local community plays an active role in protecting these migratory birds. Villagers form committees annually to safeguard the birds, ensuring that trees are preserved and hunting activities are prevented. This collaborative approach has fostered a harmonious relationship between the residents and the avian visitors. Bird Watching: The sanctuary offers an excellent opportunity for bird enthusiasts to observe various migratory species in their natural habitat. Ecological Studies: Researchers and students can engage in ecological studies, focusing on wetland ecosystems and migratory patterns. Cultural Interaction: Visitors can interact with the local community to learn about their conservation efforts and the significance of the sanctuary.
