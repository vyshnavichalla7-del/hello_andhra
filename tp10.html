Araku valley

Overview

Araku Valley is a picturesque hill station located in the Eastern Ghats of Visakhapatnam district, Andhra Pradesh. It is renowned for its scenic landscapes, pleasant climate, rich tribal culture, and nearby ancient Buddhist monuments. Key Details: Buddhist Monuments Nearby: The Araku region encompasses several ancient Buddhist sites, including the Bavikonda, Bojjana Konda, and Thotlakonda Buddhist complexes. These sites date back to around the 3rd century BCE to the 2nd century CE and feature stupas, monastic cells, prayer halls, rock-cut caves, relics, sculptures, and inscriptions. They reflect the rich heritage of Theravada Buddhism in the region. Borra Caves: Located about 35 km from Araku Valley, these limestone caves are famous for their stalactite and stalagmite formations millions of years old. The caves have religious significance and natural beauty, attracting many tourists. Tribal Culture: Araku Valley is inhabited by tribal communities who preserve unique traditions, crafts, and festivals, offering visitors cultural experiences like tribal dance and handicrafts.
