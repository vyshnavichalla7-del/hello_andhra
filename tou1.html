Nalamalla hills

History

The Nallamala Hills in the Prakasam district are part of the larger Nallamala Forest, a significant stretch of the Eastern Ghats. The hills are home to Bhairavakona waterfalls, which are a key attraction in the area, and the Yerragondapalem mandal, where a 15th-century Telugu inscription was recently discovered. The hills' rich history also includes a significant 350-year-old stepwell at Mylacherla, which serves as a source of water and a symbol of communal heritage, according to The Hans India.
