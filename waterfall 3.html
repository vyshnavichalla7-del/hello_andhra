Paritala waterfalls

overview

Paritala Waterfalls, also called Kondapalli waterfalls or Paritala Hanuman waterfalls, are located near Paritala village and Kondapalli forest, close to Vijayawada in Krishna district, Andhra Pradesh. These are small seasonal cascades nestled within the lush, wooded Kondapalli hills, offering a beautiful natural getaway. The nearby Paritala Hanuman temple is a notable landmark, adding spiritual significance to the visit. The area is favored for short trekking trips through rocky and forested terrain, with a rugged trail that may be slippery and challenging. Water flow peaks during the monsoon and post-monsoon months, making it the prime season to experience the falls. Pollution and littering near the falls have been reported, so visitors are encouraged to maintain cleanliness. Due to the somewhat difficult access and small size of the falls, the spot may be less suited for families with children.

Best time to Visit

  • Monsoon and Post-monsoon (July to October/November): Best time when the waterfalls are flowing heavily and the surroundings are green and fresh.
  • Best Times of Day: Early morning or late afternoon for pleasant weather and good lighting. Trekking in the middle of the day can be hot.
  • Avoid Summer Months: Water flow is minimal or dry in summer; also, the trails can be challenging under hot sun.
  • Take care of slippery terrain and use appropriate footwear for the trek.
