Sri Bairava Swamy Temple

History

The temple is believed to have been constructed during the 15th century under the Vijayanagara Empire by King Harihara Bukka Raya of the Sangama dynasty. It incorporates a unique blend of Vaishnavite and Shaivite traditions. The idols and sculptures are remarkable, with Lord Bhairava seated in Lalitasana, adorned with detailed ornaments and a calm demeanor, reflecting artistic excellence. The temple structure includes expansive mandapas, four grand entrance gopurams, temples for other deities like Lord Vinayaka, Lord Krishna, and a large black granite Nandi statue nearby. The temple is enclosed in a large walled compound with multiple entrances, with detailed inscriptions narrating historical donations and constructions during Vijayanagara times.

Visiting Timings

  • The temple is open typically from 6:00 AM to 8:00 PM, offering daily poojas and special ceremonies during festivals like Maha Shivaratri.
  • Visitors can participate in rituals, including abhishekam and aarti during these hours.
