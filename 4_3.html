sri penusila lakshmi narasimha swamy temple

History

  • location
    • situated in penchalakona

  • legend

    • The presiding deity is Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, worshipped here in a self-manifested (Swayambhu) form. The name Penusila comes from the Telugu words “Penu” (embrace) and “Sila” (rock), referring to the story where Goddess Lakshmi embraced Lord Narasimha to calm his fierce form after the slaying of demon Hiranyakashipu. In local lore, Goddess Lakshmi took the form of a tribal woman (Chenchu Lakshmi) to pacify the Lord, and their divine union at this site is the basis of the temple’s significance. Sage Kanva Maharshi is said to have performed deep penance here, adding spiritual power to the location..

  • architecture

    • The temple is nestled in the dense forests of the Eastern Ghats, surrounded by hills and a perennial stream. The main sanctum houses the deity in a peaceful yogic posture with lion face and human body — unique and awe-inspiring. The Rajagopuram (main entrance tower) is traditional and faces east, with stone steps and a scenic backdrop. There is a sacred tree called the Vata Vruksha in the temple complex, where devotees tie threads or offerings for blessings.
