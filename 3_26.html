mallayakonda hills

overview

  • Location:
    • Mallayyakonda Hills are located in Thamballapalle Mandal of Chittoor District, Andhra Pradesh. The area is spiritually significant and known for its scenic beauty, surrounded by valleys and forested slopes.

    • Sri Bhramarambika Sametha Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple
    Main Deity: The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva, worshipped here as Mallikarjuna Swamy. He is accompanied by Goddess Bhramarambika, a form of Parvati. The Shiva Lingam here is considered Swayambhu (self-manifested), making the site especially sacred.

    It is believed that the temple was established during the reign of Sri Krishnadevaraya, the famous king of the Vijayanagara Empire. According to local lore, a nearby hill called Sadhukonda was once used by sages for meditation. There is a belief that a mystical tunnel connects Sadhukonda to Mallayyakonda. It is said that Lord Shiva traveled through this underground passage in the form of a rat, finally manifesting as the lingam at Mallayyakonda. There is also a sacred water source nearby known as Yelaka Theertham, believed to be formed by divine grace.

    The hill is surrounded by lush greenery and cool breezes, creating a tranquil environment for spiritual seekers. Pilgrims enjoy not just the darshan but also the peaceful natural setting and panoramic views from the hilltop. .
