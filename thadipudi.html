THadipudi Reservoir

History

Thadipudi Reservoir, constructed between 1963 and 1968 across the Gosthani River in Gantyada Mandal, Vizianagaram District, Andhra Pradesh, was built primarily to provide irrigation to surrounding agricultural lands and to supply drinking water to Visakhapatnam city. It has a storage capacity of about 3.32 TMC (Thousand Million Cubic feet) of water. Agriculture: Irrigates over 15,000 acres of farmland in nearby mandals, enhancing agricultural productivity. Drinking Water: Serves as a key water source for Visakhapatnam. Tourism: The scenic reservoir attracts visitors and supports recreational activities like boating. Environmental Role: Supports local flora and fauna, contributing to the ecological balance of the region.
