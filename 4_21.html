udayagiri fort

overview

  • location
    • Udayagiri Fort is located in Udayagiri town, about 100 kilometers from Nellore city, Andhra Pradesh. It is set in a hilly terrain and sits at an altitude of around 3,000 feet above sea level.
  • history
    • The fort dates back to the 14th century. It was originally built by Langula Gajapati, a commander of the Gajapati rulers of Odisha. Later, it was captured by Krishnadevaraya of the Vijayanagara Empire during his southern campaigns. After the fall of the Vijayanagara Empire, the fort came under Golconda rulers and then the Nawabs of Arcot. The site reflects a fusion of Hindu and Islamic architecture due to the changes in rule over the centuries.
  • Architecture & Layout
  • .The fort is built on and around a hill, surrounded by a thick forest known for medicinal plants. It has multiple layers of fortification, including: Defensive walls Watchtowers Gates with inscriptions Inside the fort, you can find: Ruined temples, such as the Ranganatha Swamy Temple Old mosques, like the Chinna Masjid and Pedda Masjid Stone steps and pathways leading to the top of the hill The architecture includes stone carvings, pillared mandapas, and remnants of royal structures.
