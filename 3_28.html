puli gundu rock mountain

overview

  • Location:
    • Puligundu is a striking rock formation located about 18 km from Chittoor town in Penumuru Mandal. The rock formation consists of two large peaks that rise roughly 1,000 feet (about 300 meters) above the surrounding landscape. The name "Puligundu" means "Tiger Hill," linked to local legends involving Lord Shiva and a tiger demon. The shape of the rock is said to resemble a giant Shiva Lingam when seen from afar. .

    Puligundu is a popular trekking spot for adventure seekers. The trek involves climbing steep slopes, narrow ridges, and metal ladders installed to aid climbers. A narrow bridge connects the two peaks at the top. The trek usually takes around 2 to 3 hours depending on the pace and group.

    • spiritual significance

    At the base of Puligundu rocks is the Puli Gunteeswara Swamy Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Many devotees visit especially during festivals like Makara Sankranti to offer prayers and participate in rituals. There is also an Ayyappa temple being constructed on top of the northern rock peak, adding more religious importance to the site.
