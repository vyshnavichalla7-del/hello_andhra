sri kamakshiamba sametha sri karankanteswara temple

history

  • Location:
    • The Sri Kamakshamba Sametha Sri Karakanteswara Swamy Temple is an ancient and revered Hindu temple located in Nagari, a town in the Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh. This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva as Karakanteswara Swamy and Goddess Kamakshamba.
  • Legends:
    • The temple's presiding deity, Sri Karakanteswara Swamy, is a form of Lord Shiva worshipped alongside Goddess Kamakshamba. Devotees believe that performing rituals and prayers here can bring spiritual benefits and blessings. The temple is particularly significant during Maha Shivaratri, a major Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva.

    architecture

    The temple is built in the traditional Dravidian architectural style, characterized by its intricately carved stone pillars and detailed sculptures. It features a Shiva Lingam as the main deity, with Goddess Kamakshamba enshrined beside him. The temple complex includes a pradakshina patha (circumambulatory path) and a Nandi Mandapam facing the sanctum. The structure is adorned with stone carvings depicting various deities and mythological scenes.
