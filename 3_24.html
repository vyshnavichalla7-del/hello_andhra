sri kubera umamaheswara swamy temple

history

  • Location:
    • Situated in Thotapalyam, a locality in Chittoor town, Andhra Pradesh. Easily accessible from the main town and surrounded by a peaceful, semi-urban environment.
  • Legends:
    • Though thereâ€™s no widely documented ancient legend specific to this temple, local belief suggests that Kubera, the god of wealth, once performed penance here to seek the blessings of Lord Shiva and Parvati. In response, Lord Shiva appeared as Umamaheswara, granting Kubera divine grace. Hence, the temple is seen as a place where both spiritual elevation and material well-being are possible through devotion.

    architecture

    Built in modern Dravidian style, the temple includes traditional elements like a Vimana (tower above the sanctum), mandapams (pillared halls), and carvings of Jyotirlingas across the inner sanctum walls. The temple is clean, well-maintained, and includes small shrines for Ganapati, Navagrahas, and Kubera. It features a peaceful inner courtyard suitable for meditation and chanting.
