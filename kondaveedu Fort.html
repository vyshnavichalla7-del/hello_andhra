Kondaveedu Fort

History

Kondaveedu Fort is a historic hill fortress located in Kondaveedu village, approximately 32 km southwest of Guntur in Andhra Pradesh. Constructed in the 14th century by Prolaya Vema Reddy, it served as the capital of the Reddy dynasty from 1328 to 1482. The fort has witnessed several significant events, including battles with the Vijayanagara Empire, Golconda Sultanate, and the French and British colonial powers.
