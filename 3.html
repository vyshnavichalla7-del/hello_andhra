History

The temple's origins trace back to ancient times, with references found in Tamil Sangam literature dated to the 9th century CE, and the site itself believed to be endowed by King Thondaman and patronized by successive dynasties such as the Pallavas, Cholas, Pandyas, and especially the Vijayanagara Empire in the 14th-16th centuries . According to legend, Lord Vishnu incarnated as Sri Venkateswara during the Kali Yuga and made Tirumala his abode to protect mankind. The place is thus called "Kaliyuga Vaikuntha," and the deity is "Kaliyuga Prathyaksha Daivam" . The temple saw substantial expansion, wealth, and renovations during the Vijayanagara reign, especially under Emperor Krishnadevaraya, who contributed gold and jewels to adorn the inner sanctum, Ananda Nilayam . Despite historical challenges under Muslim rule and invasions, the temple survived, thanks to continued patronage and protection from local rulers .
