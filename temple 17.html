Sri Lakshminarayana Swamy Temple

History

The temple is an ancient structure located in Avanigadda village, Krishna district, Andhra Pradesh. It was constructed during the Chola dynasty period, approximately 1000 years ago, known for its distinct Dravidian architecture. The temple is particularly famous for its unique "Ratha" or chariot-shaped structure, which sets it apart from other temples. Several historical inscriptions on the walls describe donations by royal families and the temple's significance in the region. The temple follows Vaikhanasa Agama tradition for rituals and is maintained under the state Endowment Board and Archaeology Department.
