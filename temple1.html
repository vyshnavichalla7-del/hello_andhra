Dwaraka Tirumala

History

The temple of Dwaraka Tirumala is named after Sage Dwaraka, who discovered the self-manifested idol of Lord Venkateswara after intense penance inside an anthill (Valmikam). The lower half of this idol was submerged, so a second, full idol was brought from the main Tirupati temple and placed behind to enable full worship. According to legend, Lord Venkateswara appeared before Sage Dwaraka towards the south as the sage was praying facing north. The site is considered sacred since Satya Yuga and linked to King Aja, the grandfather of Lord Rama, who prayed here to resolve a battle. The temple is also called "Chinna Tirupati" as it serves as an alternative pilgrimage to the main Tirupati shrine.
