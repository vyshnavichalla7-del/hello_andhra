Arasavilli Temple

History

History of Arasavilli – Arasavilli is a historic village located in Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh, renowned for the Arasavilli Swamy Temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu. The temple is believed to be over 1,000 years old, constructed during ancient times by early rulers of the region, reflecting remarkable architectural and cultural significance. Legend says that the temple’s construction was divinely inspired, and it holds a unique feature where the sun’s rays fall directly on the feet of the deity twice a year (during specific festivals), symbolizing cosmic harmony. Arasavilli has been a key spiritual and cultural center in the region, attracting pilgrims, historians, and tourists, contributing to its reputation as a sacred heritage site. The village reflects a blend of tradition, faith, and history, making it an important part of Andhra Pradesh’s religious and archaeological legacy. Arasavalli is a quaint village known for its ancient temples and peaceful environment. It attracts visitors who are interested in traditional architecture and local cultural practices. The village reflects the heritage of Srikakulam with its old temples and historical significance. Located around 30 km from Srikakulam town. The Arasavilli Sun Temple is believed to be built around the 7th to 8th century A.D., making it one of the oldest sun temples in South India. The idol of Lord Surya in the temple is unique, facing east to capture the first rays of the rising sun, symbolizing energy and life.
