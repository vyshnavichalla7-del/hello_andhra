sri valli devasena subramanya swamy temple

history

  • Location:
    • Sirugurajupalem village, Puttur Mandal, Chittoor District, Andhra Pradesh
  • Legends:
    • This temple was established relatively recently, around 2012, based on local devotion to Lord Murugan and increasing demand for a spiritual center for Subramanya Swamy worship in this area. The presence of both Valli and Devasena signifies the divine marriage stories of Murugan—one with Valli through love and another with Devasena through duty, symbolizing the balance between desire and responsibility.

    architecture

    The main deity is Lord Subramanya Swamy (also known as Murugan or Karthikeya), worshipped along with his consorts Valli and Devasena. The temple is dedicated to divine love, youth, and victory—qualities associated with Lord Murugan. A tall and beautifully sculpted statue of Lord Subramanya Swamy is installed at the front, making it a prominent landmark in the region.
