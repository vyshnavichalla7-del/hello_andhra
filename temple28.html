Neetikanti veera anajaneya swamy temple

History

The temple was established about 500 years ago by the great saint Sri Vyasaraya, a prominent devotee and spiritual leader of the Vijayanagara period, who installed the idol of Lord Hanuman here. The name "Nettikanti" means "sharp-eyed" or "one who watches with the right eye," symbolizing the belief that the deity watches over his devotees with his right eye. This temple was consecrated where a dried margosa (neem) stick mysteriously sprouted as a live tree, considered a divine sign, and is believed to possess healing powers for diseases and troubles caused by negative energies. The temple features a tall five-tier Raja Gopuram covered with gold-plated kalasas and a wooden main entrance with intricate silver plate designs. It follows Vijayanagara architectural style. There is a sacred water tank in the northwest corner where devotees take holy baths believed to cure illnesses and purify the mind.
