Overview of chandragiri fort

  • Chandragiri Fort is a historical fort located in the Chandragiri suburb of Tirupati in Andhra Pradesh. It dates back to around the 11th century, originally built under the rule of Immadi Narasimha Yadava Raya. It became especially important during the Vijayanagara Empire period. At one point, the capital was shifted there when Penukonda was under threat.

  • The fort was under the Vijayanagara rulers for about three centuries. After the Battle of Talikota in 1565, parts of the empire changed hands; the Chandragiri area was later occupied by the sultan of Golconda around 1646. It later came under Mysore rule and gradually declined after the late 18th century. One key point in colonial history: in 1639, a treaty granting lands for Fort St. George (Madras) to the British was signed by the Vijayanagara king at Chandragiri.
