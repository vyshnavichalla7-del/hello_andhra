kondapalli fort

HISTORY

Built in the 14th century CE by Anna Vema Reddy of the Reddi Kingdom after capturing Kondapalli from the Mudigonda Chalukyas. It was a strategic fortress under various dynasties, including the Gajapati dynasty of Odisha, the Vijayanagara Empire, the Nawabs of Golconda, the French, and then the British.Served as a military base, trading hub, and royal leisure spot. The fort is known for its architectural remains like the Dargha Darwaja gate, Golconda Darwaza, Tanisha Mahal palace, ammunition store, granary, and an English barrack.Located atop a hill about 24 km from Vijayawada, it overlooks the Krishna River and surrounding valleys. The surrounding forest is known for medicinal plants and lightwood used to craft famous Kondapalli toys.

Best time to Visit

  • Typically open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.

Visitor advice

  • Ideal for history enthusiasts, trekkers, and those interested in handicrafts. The fort is in partial ruins but offers scenic views and cultural insights. No eateries on-site; best to carry water and snacks.
