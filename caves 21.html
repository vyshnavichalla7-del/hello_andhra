History

The main cave is Geemanugavi, stretching for about 5 km, with up to 2 km explorable by tourists. The system includes intricate stalactite and stalagmite formations, natural bridges, chandeliers, and globes, some glittering like diamonds in the light. Uudamanugavi is another sizable cave nearby, capable of accommodating around 100 people. The caves have long been a site for adventure, geological interest, and are surrounded by beautiful hillocks, springs, and paddy fields. Nearby is the Kona Ramalingeswara temple and a perennial spring, adding religious and natural significance to the site. Yadiki Caves are thought to have formed over millennia through water action on limestone and are known to host rare species of bats. Yadiki Caves are ideal for trekking, rock climbing, and eco-tourism, making them a unique exploration spot within Anantapur district for both adventure and nature lovers.
