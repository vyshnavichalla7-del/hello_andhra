Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple

History

Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple in Tirumalagiri, Krishna district, Andhra Pradesh, is a sacred hilltop temple associated with Sage Bharadwaja who performed penance here and witnessed a divine manifestation of Lord Sri Venkateswara. The legend says that the deity appeared as an anthill with twelve marks (namams) on the hill, which is considered a divine form of Lord Vishnu. The temple's history includes various improvements by local devotees and rulers ensuring daily worship and festival celebrations. It is an important pilgrimage destination marked by annual Kalyana Mahotsavam during Chaitra Purnima and attracts devotees across the region.

Visiting Timings

  • Open daily from sunrise to sunset; generally from 6:00 AM to 7:00 PM.
