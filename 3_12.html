Aradhagiri Anjaneya swamy temple

history

The name "Ardhagiri" means "Half Mountain" (Ardha = half, Giri = mountain). According to legend, when Hanuman was flying with the Sanjeevani mountain to save Lakshmana during the Ramayana, a part of the mountain fell at this place after being struck by an arrow from Bharata. This fallen piece is believed to have medicinal properties, and it became what we now call Ardhagiri Hill.

A temple tank called Sanjeevaraya Pushkarini is located at the site. Devotees believe the water from this pond has healing properties, especially for skin diseases and chronic ailments. Many take a holy dip here before visiting the temple.

The first rays of the morning sun fall directly on the feet of the deity, and gradually move upward to the head, before disappearing — a rare architectural alignment.

architecture

The Ardhagiri Veeranjaneya Swamy Temple showcases a blend of Dravidian-style temple architecture and unique natural integration with the rocky hill terrain of Ardhagiri.
