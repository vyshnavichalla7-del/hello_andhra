  • Location:

Located in Mogili Village, approximately 22 km west of Chittoor on the Chennaiâ€“Mumbai highway, the temple is renowned for its architectural beauty and spiritual significance.
  • Legends:
    • The temple is often called Dakshina Kailasam because it resembles the famous Kailasa temple in both architecture and spiritual importance. It holds a vital place in the cultural and religious landscape of the region.

    architecture

    The temple has a unique Trimurti alignment, where during the circumambulation, devotees can see idols of Dakshinamurthi (a form of Shiva), Vishnu, and Brahma positioned around the sanctum. It features three large Gopurams (gateway towers) adorned with intricate sculptures. Apart from Lord Shiva, there are idols of Goddess Kamakshi (Parvati), Chandeswara, Virabhadra, Ganapati, Bhairava, Kartikeya, Mahishasuramardini, Chandrasekhara, and Surya.
