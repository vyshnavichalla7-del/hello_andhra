Baruva Beach

Historical Significance

Colonial Era Seaport: During British colonial rule, Baruva served as an important seaport until 1948.
1917 Shipwreck Memorial: A commemorative pillar stands on the beach to honor a cargo ship that sank nearby in 1917. Lighthouse History: An old lighthouse once stood on the coast until the year 2000, marking Baruva's maritime heritage.< Golden Sands: The beach is known for its golden sands and clear waters, making it ideal for leisurely walks and relaxation. Fishing and Birdwatching: The swampy seashore, locally called "beela," is well-known for duck shooting and fishing. Cultural Sites: Nearby attractions include the Sri Janardhana Swamy Temple and Sri Kotilingeswara Swamy Temple, offering spiritual experiences.
