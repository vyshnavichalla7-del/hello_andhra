Aluru kona ranganatha swamy temple

History

The temple was built in 1334 CE by Yerrama Timmaraju, a feudatory of Vijayanagara King Bukka Raya I, who donated large tracts of land to the temple to support its upkeep. Situated on top of a hillock, the temple features a three-tiered rajagopuram facing east, with traditional temple elements such as Balipeedam (sacrificial altar), Dwaja Sthambam (flagpole), Deepa Sthambam (lamp post), and a Garuda shrine immediately after the gopuram. The presiding deity, Lord Ranganatha, is in a reclining pose (Anantasayana) approximately 6 feet long, accompanied by Sri Devi, Bhu Devi, and Brahma in the sanctum. A natural spring flows year-round near the temple between rocks, considered sacred. The temple also hosts an annual Brahmotsavam festival starting on Chaitra Sudha Pournami (full moon). The temple complex covers over 51 acres and maintains guest house facilities and accommodations for devotees.
