Mandasa Vasudeva Perumal Temple

History

History of Mandasa Temple – Mandasa Temple, located in Srikakulam district of Andhra Pradesh, is an ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is believed to have been constructed during the reign of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, around the 11th century. It holds historical importance as part of the region’s rich cultural and religious heritage. The temple architecture reflects traditional Kalinga and Dravidian styles, with beautifully carved pillars and stone structures. Mandasa Temple has long been a spiritual center for Shaivite devotees and a significant pilgrimage site in the area. Over time, it has attracted visitors for both its religious significance and architectural beauty. This temple is dedicated to Lord Vasudeva (Krishna). It is a significant religious site where devotees visit to offer prayers, especially during Janmashtami. The temple reflects classic Dravidian architectural elements and stands as an important cultural landmark. Situated in Mandastha village, near Srikakulam. This temple has its origins in the medieval period, believed to have been constructed around the 10th to 12th century A.D. during the reign of local rulers who were great patrons of Vaishnavism.
