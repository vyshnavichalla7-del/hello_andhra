Guntupalli Buddist Caves

History

The Guntupalli Buddhist Caves, also known as the Guntupalli Group of Buddhist Monuments, are an ancient Buddhist site located near Kamavarapukota in the West Godavari district of Andhra Pradesh, India. Situated approximately 40 km from Eluru, the site is nestled amidst lush greenery and rocky hills, offering a serene environment for exploration. Dating back to the 2nd–3rd centuries BCE, the Guntupalli caves represent the Hinayana (early) phase of Buddhism. The site comprises rock-cut caves, monasteries, stupas, and a chaitya hall—a prayer hall with a unique stone entrance resembling wooden architecture. The chaitya hall is notable for its simplicity and early Buddhist monastic architecture. Surrounding the main structure are over 30 smaller votive stupas, constructed using both natural and brick materials, arranged on a terrace. Excavations at the site have uncovered relic caskets containing gold, silver, crystal beads, and a bronze image of Padmapani, indicating the site's historical significance.
