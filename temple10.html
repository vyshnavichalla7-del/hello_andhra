sri mogaltur veerabhadra swamy temple

history

Sri Veerabhadra Swamy Temple near Mogalthur in West Godavari district is a revered temple dedicated to Lord Veerabhadra, an incarnation of Lord Shiva. While the most famous Veerabhadra Swamy Temple is located on Devakuta Parvatham hill amidst the Godavari River near Pattiseema, known for its connection to the legend of Sati and Shiva and the powerful Rudra Tandava dance of Veerabhadra, temples dedicated to Veerabhadra Swamy exist at various places including Mogalthur. The Pattiseema temple is noted for its massive monolithic Nandi statue and rich Vijayanagara architecture, with numerous sub-shrines to other deities like Bhadrakali, Lakshmi Ganapathi, and Subramanya Swamy. Devotees visit for blessings and participate in major festivals like Maha Shivaratri, where elaborate rituals and spiritual activities take place. The temple in the region is an important spiritual and cultural landmark for the devotees of Lord Shiva.
