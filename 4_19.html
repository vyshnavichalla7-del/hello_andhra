somasila dam

overview

  • location
    • Somasila Dam is built across the Penna (Pennar) River in Ananthasagaram Mandal, Nellore District, Andhra Pradesh. It is situated in a scenic region surrounded by small hills and is one of the most important water projects in the region.
  • more info

    • Irrigation: The primary purpose of Somasila Dam is to provide water for irrigation. It supports thousands of acres of agricultural land in the Penna delta and nearby regions. Drinking Water: Supplies water to towns and villages including Nellore city and others. Flood Control: Helps regulate the flow of the Penna River and prevents flooding during heavy monsoons. Inter-Basin Link: It is part of the Telugu Ganga Project, which diverts Krishna River water through multiple reservoirs (including Kandaleru) to Chennai for drinking purposes.

  • Structure & Capacity
    • The dam is a combination of earth-fill and gravity structure. Full Reservoir Level (FRL): Around 100.58 meters above mean sea level. Storage Capacity: About 78 TMC (Thousand Million Cubic Feet). Water Spread Area: Over 212 square kilometers when the reservoir is full. The dam includes sluice gates and spillways to control water release during heavy inflows.

Visiting Timings

  • Morning: 5:00 AM to 12:30 PM
  • Evening: 3:00 PM to 7:30 PM
  • The ideal time to visit Somasila Dam is October to March, when the weather is cooler and the reservoir is likely to be filled. During monsoons, water levels rise significantly, offering a majestic view of the dam in action.
